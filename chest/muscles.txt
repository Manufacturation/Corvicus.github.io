    <div class="section_title">Clinical relevance for each muscle connected to the chest</div>
    <div class="grid-container3">
        <div class="item4 all">
          <div class="image_muscle">
            <img src="images/coracobrachialis.png">
            <div class="under"><span class="under_name">Coracobrachialis</span></div>
          </div>
          <div class="origin">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Origin</b></i>: tip (apex) if the ciracoid process of scapula</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="insertion">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Insertion</b></i>: middle third of the medial surface and border of the humerus</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="action">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Action</b></i>: weakly adducts arm (flexion unsubstantieted), aids in stabilizing humerus</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="nerve">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Nerve</b></i>: musculocutaneous nerve (C6,C7)</span>
          </div>
        </div>

        <div class="item5 all">
          <div class="image_muscle">
            <img src="images/deltoid.png">
            <div class="under"><span class="under_name">Deltoid</span></div>
          </div>
          <div class="origin">
            <span class="intelegent_text" style="font-size: 1em;"><i><b>Origin</b></i>: anterior position-anterior border and superior surface of the lateral third of the clavicle; middle portion-lateral border of the acromion process; posterior portion-extends and laterally rotates arm</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="insertion">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Insertion</b></i>: deltoid tuberosity, on the middle of the lateral surface of the shaft of the humerus</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="action">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Action</b></i>: anterior portion-flexes and medially rotates arm; middle portion-abducts arm; pposterior portion-extends and laterally rotates arm</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="nerve">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Nerve</b></i>: axillary nerve (C5,C6)</span>
          </div>
        </div>
        
        <div class="item6 all">
          <div class="image_muscle">
            <img src="images/intercostales_externi.png">
            <div class="under"><span class="under_name">Intercostales Externi</span></div>
          </div>
          <div class="origin">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Origin</b></i>: lower margin of upper eleven ribs</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="insertion">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Insertion</b></i>: superior border of rib below(each muscle fiber runs obliquely and inserts toword the costal cartilage)</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="action">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Action</b></i>: draw ventral part of ribs upward, increasing the volume of the thoracic cavity for inspiration</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="nerve">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Nerve</b></i>: intercostal nerves</span>
          </div>
        </div>
          
        <div class="item7 all">
          <div class="image_muscle">
            <img src="images/intercostales_interni.png">
            <div class="under"><span class="under_name">Intercostales Interni</span></div>
          </div>
          <div class="origin">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Origin</b></i>: from the cartilages to the angles of hte upper elebem ribs</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="insertion">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Insertion</b></i>: superior border of the rib below (each muscle fiber runs obliquely and inserts away from the costal cartilage)</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="action">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Action</b></i>: draw ventral part of ribs downward, decreasing the volume of the thoracic cavity for expiration</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="nerve">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Nerve</b></i>: intercostal nerves</span>
          </div>
        </div>
           
        <div class="item8 all">
          <div class="image_muscle">
            <img src="images/pectoralis_major.png">
            <div class="under"><span class="under_name">Pectoralis major</span></div>
          </div>
          <div class="origin">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Origin</b></i>: clavicular part-medial half of the clavicle; sternocostal part-sternum, upper six costal cartilages, aponeurosis of external olique</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="insertion">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Insertion</b></i>: lateral lip of intertubercuular (bicipital) groove of the humerus</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="action">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Action</b></i>: both parts adduct, medially rotate arm; clavicular part flexes arm from full extension; sternocostal part extends the flexed arm</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="nerve">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Nerve</b></i>: medial and lateral pectoral nerves (C5-C8, T1)</span>
          </div>
        </div>
           
        <div class="item9 all">
          <div class="image_muscle">
            <img src="images/pectoralis_minor.png">
            <div class="under"><span class="under_name">Pectoralis minor</span></div>
          </div>
          <div class="origin">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Origin</b></i>: external surfaces if the third, fourth and fifth ribs</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="insertion">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Insertion</b></i>: coracoid process of the scapula</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="action">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Action</b></i>: draws scapula forward and downard, raises ribs in forced inspiration</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="nerve">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Nerve</b></i>: medial pectoral nerve (C8,T1)</span>
          </div>
        </div>
           
        <div class="item10 all">
          <div class="image_muscle">
            <img src="images/platysma.png">
            <div class="under"><span class="under_name">Platysma</span></div>
          </div>
          <div class="origin">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Origin</b></i>: substaneous fascia of upper one-fourth of chest just below the clavicle</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="insertion">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Insertion</b></i>: substaneous fascia and muscles of chin and jaw, mandible</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="action">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Action</b></i>: depresses and draws lower lip laterally, draws up skin of chest, depresses mandible</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="nerve">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Nerve</b></i>: cervical brawnch of facial nerve</span>
          </div>
        </div>
           
        <div class="item11 all">
          <div class="image_muscle">
            <img src="images/serratus_posterior_superior.png">
            <div class="under"><span class="under_name">Serratus Posterior Superior</span></div>
          </div>
          <div class="origin">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Origin</b></i>: ligamentum nuchae, spinous processes of seventh cervical and first few thoracic vertebrae</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="insertion">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Insertion</b></i>: upper borders of the second through fifth tibs lateral to their angles</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="action">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Action</b></i>: raises  ribs in inspiration</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="nerve">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Nerve</b></i>: T1-T4</span>
          </div>
        </div>
           
        <div class="item12 all">
          <div class="image_muscle">
            <img src="images/sternocleidomastoid.png">
            <div class="under"><span class="under_name">Sternocleidomastoid</span></div>
          </div>
          <div class="origin">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Origin</b></i>: sternal head-manubrium of sternum<br>
            clavicular head-meidal part of clavicle</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="insertion">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Insertion</b></i>: mastoid process of temporal bone, lateral half of superior nuchal line of occipital bone</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="action">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Action</b></i>: one side-bends neck laterally, rotates head to opposite side<br>both sides together-flexes neck, draws head ventrally and elevates chin, draws sternum superiorly in deep inspiration</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="nerve">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Nerve</b></i>: spinal part of accessory nerve (C2, C3)</span>
          </div>
        </div>

        <div class="item13 all">
          <div class="image_muscle">
            <img src="images/subclavius.png">
            <div class="under"><span class="under_name">Subclavious</span></div>
          </div>
          <div class="origin">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Origin</b></i>: junction of the first rib with it's costal cartilage</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="insertion">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Insertion</b></i>: groove on the inferior (lower) suurface of the clavice</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="action">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Action</b></i>: depresses clavicle, draws shoulder forward and downward, streadis clavicle duringg movements of shoulder girdle</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="nerve">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Nerve</b></i>: c5,c6</span>
          </div>
        </div>

        <div class="item14 all">
          <div class="image_muscle">
            <img src="images/subscapularis.png">
            <div class="under"><span class="under_name">Subscapularis</span></div>
          </div>
          <div class="origin">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Origin</b></i>: subscapular fossa on the anterior surface of scapula</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="insertion">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Insertion</b></i>: lesset tuberosity (tubercle) of the humerus, ventral part of the capsule of the shoulder joint</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="action">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Action</b></i>: medially rotates arm, stabilizes glednohumeral joint</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="nerve">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Nerve</b></i>: upper and lower subscapular nerves (C5,C6)</span>
          </div>
        </div>

        <div class="item15 all">
          <div class="image_muscle">
            <img src="images/transversus_thoracis.png">
            <div class="under"><span class="under_name">Transversus Thoracis</span></div>
          </div>
          <div class="origin">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Origin</b></i>: inner surface of lower portion of sternum and adjacent costal cartilages</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="insertion">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Insertion</b></i>: inner surfaces of costal cartilages of the second through sixth ribs</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="action">
            <span class="intelegent_text">draws venrtal part of ribs downward, decreasing the volume of the thoracic cavity for forceful expiration</span>
          </div>
          <hr>
          <div class="nerve">
            <span class="intelegent_text"><i><b>Nerve</b></i>: intercostal nerves</span>
